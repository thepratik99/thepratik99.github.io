Q: How does ridership change with seasons?

  According to LA's weather history, there are high chances of rain in winter, from December to March. Thus, the average number of trips per day decreases in that time period. However, the average duration of a trip does not change that much. As we can see in the graph, the number of trips done by Walk-ups is pretty close to the number of trips done by Monthly pass holders from August to October. After that, we can see a decrease in the number of trips done by Monthly pass holders from November to March. On the other hand, the number of trips done by walk-ups changes with the same pattern as total trips from November to March. Change in the number of trips done by Flex pass users has a similar pattern to the total trips.

Q: What is the breakdown of Trip Route Category-Passholder type combinations?

  According to the graph above, only about 5% of the trips done by Monthly pass or flex pass holders are Round trips. People who purchase a Monthly pass or a Flex pass tend to use the bike share as a part of their regular commute. Thus, they always make one-way trips from their home to work and vice-versa. On the other hand, about 20% of the trips done by walk-ups are Round trips. One way trips cost more money to the company because they have to transport bikes from station to station in order to make sure that there are enough bikes at every station. Round trips start and end at the same station. If the company makes a round trip cheaper than a one way trip for walk-ups, it can increase the percentage of round trips done by walk-ups.
